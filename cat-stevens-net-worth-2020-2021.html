Early Life

Cat Stevens was born in 1948, on the 21st of July, to a family of Greek descendants. His father was initially from Cyprus but had Greek origins. Stevens’ mother had her ancestry in Sweden. Quite similar to most other stories about immigrants, Cat Stevens and his family of five (his parents also had two other children), operated a restaurant called Moulin Rouge.

The restaurant was French in nature and also operated as their place of domicile. The entire family settled in at London, England. When Stevens (whose original name was Steven Georgiou), was less than ten years old, his parents decided to get a divorce. However, in the interest of maintaining the family and their restaurant, lived together and maintained the household as well.

Cat Stevens’ father was an orthodox member of the Greek society, while his mother decided to follow the path of the Baptists. Nonetheless, Stevens had attended a Roman Catholic school which was near his area. Stevens took an early interest in the piano and taught himself all the different chord shapes. This was due to the fact that nobody else in the family had much of an idea about music.

The music of the Beatles ultimately inspired Cat Stevens to pick up a guitar and start practising. In an attempt to escape the problems in his life, he often resorted to going up to the rooftops and listening to music which streamed through from the nearest street, called Denmark Street. Denmark Street, at the time, was the centre of the music industry which was established in Britain.

Career

Cat Stevens decided to pursue a career in music and started by plying his trade in the pubs and bars of London. His initial idea was to incorporate his talents into a band. However, he soon dropped the idea as his creativity was being stifled. Thinking that his birth name was not exactly one which would be remembered by patrons, he decided to name himself as Cat Stevens. At the age of 18, he caught the eye of the right people and recorded his first demo. This set off his career, as he soon started to tour the world with the likes of Jimi Hendrix.

At the time, his popularity was only in Europe. However, he was almost unknown in the Americas. This changed after he signed a record deal in 1970, with Island Records. His popularity, as well as his message of changing the world for the better, started spreading across the Americas as well. This garnered him a large amount of popularity.

Awards and Achievements

Cat Stevens has shown his artistic achievements, winning awards as a songwriter from his infantile years as a singer and songwriter from ASCAP. His first album release in the USA sold over a million copies, and a majority of his songs have reached the top of the Billboards.
