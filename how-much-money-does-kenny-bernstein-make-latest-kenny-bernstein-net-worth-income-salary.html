Kenny Bernstein (born September 6, 1944) is a former drag racer and team owner in the NASCAR and IndyCar series in the United States. Because of his achievements in the Budweiser King Funny Car and Dragster, he has earned the moniker “Bud King.” He has also been dubbed “The King of Speed” since he was the first driver to reach speeds of more than 300 miles per hour in the standing-start quarter mile in a single lap. NHRA driver Bernstein was the owner of King Racing, which fielded cars in several different racing series, including IndyCar and NASCAR, and for which he raced in the NHRA. The next year, Bernstein resigned from full-time racing and hired his son Brandon to drive the Bud King Top Fuel dragster. After Brandon sustained a serious injury, Bernstein returned to the track to complete the season in Brandon’s stead. Aside from a short comeback to Funny Car in 2006, Bernstein did not race again and instead focused his efforts on running his team until the conclusion of the 2011 season, when he decided to retire from the sport completely.
