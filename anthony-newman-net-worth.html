FAQs about Anthony Newman

Who is Anthony Newman?

Anthony Newman is a renowned American classical musician, celebrated for his expertise as an organist, harpsichordist, and Bach specialist.

What instruments does he play?

He plays the organ, harpsichord, pedal harpsichord, piano, and has also composed for various other instruments.

Has he worked with any notable musicians?

Yes, he has collaborated with famous musicians like Itzhak Perlman, Leonard Bernstein, and Wynton Marsalis, among others.

What is Anthony Newman’s educational background?

Newman studied at the École Normale de Musique in Paris and holds degrees from Mannes School of Music and Harvard University.

Where has he taught music?

He has taught at prestigious institutions such as The Juilliard School, Indiana University, and SUNY Purchase.

What controversy surrounds his performances?

His fast tempos and unique interpretations of Bach have been both criticized and praised, sparking significant debate in the classical music community.

What are some achievements in his recording career?

Newman has recorded a vast array of Bach’s keyboard works and has released recordings on labels like Sony and Deutsche Grammophon.

What roles does Anthony Newman hold in music organizations?

What is his approach to Baroque performance?

Newman advocates for historically informed performances, often using original instruments and techniques from the Baroque era.
